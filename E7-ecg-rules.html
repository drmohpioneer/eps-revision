E7 - ECG interpretation rules

Four "read in context" rules, each an EXCEPT question with one impostor. Exam anchors: Q3 low voltage NOT pregnancy, Q4 LAD NOT secundum ASD, Q5 pregnancy PR SHORTENS, Q6 neonate short QTc <= 280 is abnormal.

1. Low voltage: click a cause group to reveal

Definition: QRS < 5 mm in ALL limb leads and/or < 10 mm in ALL precordial leads. Sort every cause into two buckets: something insulating the signal, or less muscle making it.

Bucket A

Insulation heart to electrode

click to reveal

  • Pericardial effusion (fluid around the heart)
  • Obesity (fat layer)
  • COPD / emphysema (air, a poor conductor)
  • Pleural effusion
  • Anasarca (generalised oedema)
Bucket B

Less / infiltrated muscle

click to reveal

  • Previous massive MI (large dead scar)
  • Amyloidosis (low voltage + THICK LV on echo = the pearl)
  • Sarcoidosis
  • Haemochromatosis (iron)
Also know

Two more + the impostor

click to reveal

  • Myxoedema (hypothyroidism)
  • Constrictive pericarditis
  • NOT pregnancy (Q3 = C): pregnancy does NOT lower voltage
Q3 trap: constrictive pericarditis, massive MI, amyloid/sarcoid/iron infiltration and myxoedema all lower voltage. Pregnancy is the impostor.

2. Axis determination tool - toggle leads I and aVF

Set the net QRS direction in lead I and lead aVF. The vector rotates to the result and the causes of that deviation appear.

Lead I (net QRS)
Lead aVF (net QRS)

Normal axis

-30 to +90 degrees

The two-thumb rule: both up = normal; leaving each other (I up, aVF down) = LEFT; reaching each other (I down, aVF up) = RIGHT; both down = extreme. ASD trap: secundum = RIGHT (RSR' in V1), primum = LEFT.

3. Normal adult vs pregnancy vs neonate - side by side

Normal adult

Axis Normal (-30 to +90)
Rate 60 to 100
PR 120 to 200 ms
V1 to V3 T waves Upright
V1 R wave Small (LV dominant)
QTc ~ up to 440 to 450 ms

Pregnancy

Axis Shifts LEFT (diaphragm up)
Rate Increased (sinus tachy)
PR SHORTENED (not prolonged - Q5)
Lead III small Q + inverted T (positional)
V1 to V3 T-wave changes
Ectopy More atrial/ventricular

Neonate

Axis RIGHT (RV dominant at birth)
V1 R wave Dominant R (thick RV)
V1 to V3 T waves Inverted (juvenile = normal)
Intervals Relatively short PR / QRS
Inferior / left precordial small q waves
QTc ~400s ms; <= 280 ms is ABNORMAL (Q6)
Impostors: pregnancy PR does not prolong (it shortens, Q5 = A). A neonatal QTc <= 280 ms is abnormal, not normal (Q6 = D). Note: right axis + right-precordial T inversion is NORMAL in a newborn but an ARVC flag in an adult.
