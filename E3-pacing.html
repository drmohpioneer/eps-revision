Cardiac Pacing

A pacemaker fixes a heart that is too slow. "Too slow" splits into two problems: a weak generator (sick sinus) or a cut wire (AV block). Pick the mode that matches the problem. Everything below is interactive.

The big picture — two problems, two fixes

Problem 1 · Weak generator
Sick sinus syndrome (SSS)
SA node under-fires; wiring below is fine. Kick the atrium and let it conduct down normally.
Pace atrium → NARROW QRS.
Problem 2 · Cut wire
AV block
Atria fire fine, signal can't reach ventricles. Pace the ventricle, ideally in step with the atrium.
Pace ventricle → WIDE (LBBB-like) QRS.
Third question the device asks: can the patient speed up on exertion? If not (chronotropic incompetence), add a sensor = the R in AAIR/DDDR.

NBG code decoder — pick a mode, read the 5 letters

Note (NBG code): position I = chamber Paced, II = chamber Sensed, III = response to sensing (I inhibited, T triggered, D dual), IV = R for rate modulation, V = multisite pacing.

Note (magnet): a magnet placed over a pacemaker forces ASYNCHRONOUS pacing (VOO or DOO) at a fixed magnet rate, useful to test capture or override oversensing.
