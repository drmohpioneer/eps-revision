🔄E1 · SVT mechanisms
Two engines, and the RP interval names the SVT.
  • Re-entry (a loop) vs automaticity (a focus)
  • SHORT RP = AVNRT / orthodromic AVRT
  • LONG RP = atypical AVNRT / PJRT / AT
⚡E2 · Pre-excitation / WPW
Delta = pathway conducts DOWN; localise on the sinus ECG.
  • V1 up = left, down = right; then inferior + lead I
  • SCD risk: SPERRI ≤ 250 ms in AF
  • Concealed pathway = no delta
🔋E3 · Pacing
Pick the mode from the problem.
  • SSS → DDDR, AV block → DDD, AF+slow → VVIR
  • Alternating RBBB/LBBB = Class I
  • Reversible brady = do NOT pace
❤️E4 · CRT
Fixes dyssynchrony, not rate.
  • EF ≤ 35 + LBBB + QRS ≥ 150, sinus
  • LV lead via CS into the posterolateral vein
  • Need > 98% BiV capture (AF/PVCs steal it)
📈E5 · VT & ARVC
Regular wide tachy = VT until proven otherwise.
  • RVOT (adenosine) vs fascicular (verapamil)
  • Digoxin / CPVT = bidirectional VT
  • ARVC: epsilon + T-inv V1-3, no sport, ICD
🧬E6 · Channelopathies + SCD
Channels, triggers, and the QT.
  • LQT1 exercise / LQT2 emotion / LQT3 rest
  • Brugada = SCN5A loss, coved ST V1-2
  • Torsades → Mg; CPVT → BB + flecainide
📏E7 · ECG rules
Four EXCEPT lists, one impostor each.
  • Low voltage (NOT pregnancy)
  • Axis: secundum ASD = RAD, primum = LAD
  • Pregnancy PR SHORTENS; neonate RAD
💊E8 · AF & anticoagulation
Score it, dose it, know the traps.
  • CHA2DS2-VASc: men ≥ 2, women ≥ 3
  • NOAC doses (apixaban best for GI bleed)
  • Valvular AF (mech valve / mod-sev MS) = warfarin
